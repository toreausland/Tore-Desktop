About Aurelian Manufacturing

Aurelian Manufacturing AS (org.nr 835 679 632) is building Norway's first purpose-built autonomous CNC manufacturing facility. The company operates a "Production as a Service" model, delivering high-mix, low-volume precision machining for defense, energy, maritime, and critical infrastructure sectors.

The facility is located in Våler, Østfold — a purpose-built 2,635 m² production hall by Norbygg on a 30,000 m² site, designed from the ground up for autonomous operation.

The structural problem: Norway faces a documented shortage of 39,000 skilled workers (NAV 2025), creating a persistent bottleneck in precision manufacturing capacity. Aurelian's greenfield approach enables design choices — 24/7 autonomous scheduling, sub-linear staffing, integrated digital thread — that are structurally difficult for existing operations to retrofit.

Market Opportunity

Three structural forces converge to create a compelling market window for autonomous precision manufacturing in Norway.

Labor Shortage
Norway's documented shortage of 39,000 skilled workers (NAV 2025) creates a structural bottleneck. CNC operators are among the hardest-to-fill positions, making autonomous manufacturing a necessity rather than a luxury.
Defense Budget Growth
Norwegian and Nordic defense budgets are at historic highs, with a projected combined spend of 1,624 billion NOK. This drives sustained demand for domestic precision manufacturing with defense certifications (AQAP 2110).
Energy Transition
Equinor, Aker, and Vattenfall are investing heavily in offshore wind and energy infrastructure, creating demand for NORSOK-compatible precision components from certified Norwegian suppliers.

Sources: NAV Bedriftsundersøkelsen 2025, CNC Benchmark & Competitive Landscape (VDR 03.02), Market Trends & Projections (VDR 03.06).

Business Model & Revenue Logic

Aurelian operates a "Production as a Service" model: customers pay per machine-hour for precision CNC machining, with no upfront capital commitment. Revenue is a direct function of three variables.

Revenue formula: CNC count × 8,760 hours/year × utilization% × NOK 3,000/hour

3,000 NOK
Hourly rate (conservative floor)
8,760 h
Available hours per CNC/year
0.8 FTE
Staff per CNC (design target at scale)

The hourly rate of NOK 3,000 represents a conservative midpoint within the observed industry range of NOK 2,500–3,500 (CNC Benchmark, VDR 03.02). The design target of 0.8 FTE per CNC at scale is enabled by greenfield automation design, 24/7 autonomous scheduling, and integrated tool management.

Customer Program (50/50 profit-sharing): Qualified customers committing more than 100,000 hours annually receive standard pricing at NOK 3,000/hour. Above 45% utilization, marginal profit is split 50/50 between Aurelian and the customer. This aligns incentives and builds long-term partnerships.

Note: The staffing ratio of 0.8 FTE/CNC is a design target for a pre-revenue company, not an observed metric. Industry observed average is approximately 2.5 staff per CNC (VDR 03.02).
