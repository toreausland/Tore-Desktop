3.1 Detailed Impact Analysis

Discrepancy #1 & #2: Machine count and ramp-up

VDR 03.06 uses an older 4-stage ramp-up model (4+4+6+6 machines from 2028–2031). REV6 established a cleaner 2-round model: 5 CNC funded by Seed, followed by 15 CNC in 3×5 tranches funded by Serie A. This affects:

  • Chapter 6.2 (ramp-up table)
  • Chapter 6.4 (result profile per year)
  • Chapter 7.1 (CAPEX phasing table)
  • Chapter 5.2 (staffing model table)

Discrepancy #3: Total cost

The ~10 MNOK cost difference stems from different depreciation assumptions, admin costs, and facility lease terms between the pre-REV6 model and the final REV6 version. REV6 value of ~92.75 MNOK includes:

Discrepancy #4: Admin payroll

VDR 03.06 uses ~1.05 MNOK per admin FTE. REV6 uses 1.4 MNOK per admin FTE, reflecting full employer cost including social contributions, pension, and overhead. The REV6 figure is the correct, fully loaded rate.

The §2.2 rule requires that Aurelian's numbers (design targets for a pre-revenue company) must never be presented side-by-side with observed industry data without clear labeling.
