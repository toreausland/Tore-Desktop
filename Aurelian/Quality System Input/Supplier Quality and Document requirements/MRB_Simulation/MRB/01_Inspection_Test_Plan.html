1. Purpose & Scope

This Inspection and Test Plan (ITP) defines the inspection, examination, and testing activities required during the manufacture and assembly of the Subsea Connector Assembly, Size 300 (Upfacing), fabricated from Super Duplex Stainless Steel UNS S32750, for the Barents Sea Development Phase 2 project.

The ITP covers all manufacturing stages from material receipt through final release, specifying hold points, witness points, and documentation requirements in accordance with the purchase order and applicable codes and standards.

5. Notes

  • Hold Points (H): Work shall not proceed beyond the hold point until the designated representative has verified and signed off the relevant documentation. NSS shall be notified minimum 72 hours in advance of scheduled hold points.
  • Witness Points (W): NSS shall be notified minimum 48 hours in advance. If NSS representative does not attend and does not provide written waiver, the activity may proceed after the notification period has elapsed.
  • Third-Party Inspection: Third-party inspector (DNV GL or as agreed) shall attend witness points for hydrostatic testing and radiographic testing as specified above.
  • NDE Personnel: All NDE personnel shall be qualified and certified to ISO 9712 Level II minimum. RT operators shall hold Level II certification for the applicable method.
  • Calibration: All measuring and test equipment shall have valid calibration certificates traceable to national standards (ISO 10012). Calibration certificates shall be included in the MRB.
  • NCR Process: Any non-conformance identified during manufacturing or inspection shall be documented on a Non-Conformance Report (NCR) and processed per AM-QP-NCR-001. All NCRs shall be included in the MRB.
  • Deviations: Any deviation from this ITP requires written approval from both Aurelian QA and NSS QA representative prior to implementation.
