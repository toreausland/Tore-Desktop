1. Purpose & Scope

This procedure defines the requirements for the hydrostatic pressure test of the Subsea Connector Assembly, Size 300 (Upfacing), fabricated from Super Duplex Stainless Steel UNS S32750. The test is performed in accordance with ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code Section VIII Division 1 and the purchase order requirements to verify the pressure-containing integrity of the assembly prior to delivery.

The test shall demonstrate that the connector assembly can withstand 1.5 times the design pressure without leakage, visible deformation, or other signs of distress.

5. Test Procedure

  1. 1
    Visual Inspection — Pre-Test
    Perform visual inspection of the connector assembly prior to test. Verify all welds, flanges, and surfaces are free from visible defects. Confirm all NDE activities are complete and accepted. Record pre-test condition in the inspection log.
  2. 2
    Install Test Fittings, Blinds, and Gauges
    Install test blinds, end closures, and pressure fittings. Connect pressure gauges, chart recorder, and safety relief valve. Verify all connections are tight and properly torqued. Confirm the safety relief valve is set at 545 barg.
  3. 3
    Fill with Test Medium and Vent All High Points
    Fill the assembly with clean fresh water containing 200 ppm biocide inhibitor. Vent all high points to remove entrapped air. Continue filling until water flows freely from all vent points. Close vent valves. Record water temperature.
  4. 4
    Pressurize to 50% Test Pressure — Preliminary Hold
    Gradually pressurize to 50% of test pressure (approximately 259 barg). Hold for 5 minutes minimum. Perform visual inspection of all joints, connections, and the assembly body. Check for leaks. If satisfactory, proceed to Step 5.
  5. 5
    Pressurize to 100% Test Pressure (517 barg)
    Continue pressurization gradually to the full test pressure of 517 barg. Do not exceed 10 bar/min above 80% of test pressure. Isolate the test pump once pressure is stabilised. Start the chart recorder at full test pressure. Record the time, pressure, and water temperature.
  6. 6
    Hold for 30 Minutes — Record Pressure and Temperature
    Maintain test pressure for a minimum of 30 minutes. Record pressure (both gauges) and water temperature at 5-minute intervals. The chart recorder shall continuously log pressure throughout the hold period. Note any observations.
  7. 7
    Inspect All Joints, Welds, and Connections Under Pressure
    While at full test pressure, perform thorough visual inspection of all welded joints, bolted connections, seal interfaces, and test fittings. Check for any signs of leakage, weeping, sweating, or deformation. Inspection to be performed only after pressure has been stable for at least 10 minutes.
  8. 8
    Depressurize Gradually
    After successful completion of the hold period and visual inspection, depressurize the assembly gradually at a controlled rate not exceeding 20 bar/min. Do not vent rapidly. Record the final pressure reading and time.
  9. 9
    Drain and Dry
    Drain all test water from the assembly. Flush with clean fresh water if required. Dry the assembly using clean compressed air. Ensure no residual moisture remains in seal grooves, bolt holes, or internal cavities.
  10. 10
    Visual Inspection — Post-Test
    Perform final visual inspection of the assembly after depressurization. Check for any permanent deformation, cracking, or damage. Compare post-test condition to pre-test condition. Remove all test fittings and blinds. Record post-test condition.

6. Acceptance Criteria

The hydrostatic pressure test shall be considered ACCEPTABLE when all of the following criteria are met:
  • No visible leaks — No leakage, weeping, or sweating detected at any joint, weld, seal, or connection during the hold period.
  • Pressure drop ≤ 1% — The pressure drop during the 30-minute hold period shall not exceed 1% of the test pressure (i.e., maximum 5.17 bar drop from initial test pressure).
  • No permanent deformation — No visible permanent deformation, bulging, or distortion of the assembly observed during post-test visual inspection.
  • No cracking or damage — No cracks, surface damage, or other distress indications detected.

7. Safety Requirements

WARNING: Hydrostatic testing involves high-pressure operations. All personnel involved must be aware of the hazards and follow the safety requirements below.
  • Exclusion zone: Establish a barricaded exclusion zone of minimum 2 metres around the test area during pressurization and hold. Only authorised personnel may enter the exclusion zone.
  • Warning signage: Post clear warning signs: “HIGH PRESSURE TEST IN PROGRESS — DO NOT ENTER” at all access points to the exclusion zone.
  • PPE: All personnel within the test area shall wear safety glasses, steel-toe boots, and hearing protection (if pneumatic pump is used).
  • Communications: Maintain clear verbal communication between the pump operator and the test inspector at all times during pressurization.
  • Safety relief valve: The safety relief valve set at 545 barg must be installed and verified before any pressurization begins. The relief valve shall not be isolated during the test.
  • Pump isolation: Isolate the test pump from the assembly before commencing the hold period. The pump operator shall remain at the pump station throughout.
  • Emergency procedure: In case of a leak or equipment failure, immediately stop pressurization, depressurize via the bleed valve, and evacuate the exclusion zone. Report to the test supervisor.
  • Toolbox talk: Conduct a pre-test safety briefing (toolbox talk) with all personnel involved. Document attendance.
