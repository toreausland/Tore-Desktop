Section 1

Executive Summary

What has been built, what has changed, and where we are now.

What is the Digital MRB Builder?

A Manufacturing Record Book (MRB) is the complete documentation package that accompanies every manufactured product to the customer. In defence and oil & gas, this is not optional — it is a contractual and regulatory requirement. An incomplete or incorrect MRB can block shipment, trigger penalties, and jeopardize certifications.

The Digital MRB Builder is Aurelian’s in-house software system that automates the entire MRB lifecycle: from parsing customer documentation requirements at order intake, through material gate review, document collection from CNC machines and suppliers, 5-layer document validation, Certificate of Conformance generation, final MRB assembly, delivery, and 15–30+ year archival.

It is not a document management system. It is an active quality engine that enforces compliance in real time, catches errors before they reach the customer, and generates legally binding documentation automatically from validated source data.

Section 2 NEW IN V2

Key Architectural Decisions

Four strategic decisions have been made since V1. These fundamentally shape the system architecture and development approach.

Decision 1 — Build Own ERP Module

Decision: Aurelian will build its own ERP module as part of a unified platform, rather than purchasing a third-party ERP (e.g., Epicor, SAP).

Rationale: Ensures seamless integration in our digital tech stack. The ERP module and MRB Builder share one PostgreSQL database with separate schemas (erp.* and mrb.*). Internal module boundaries replace the 20 external API interfaces defined in AM-IF-001.

Impact: AM-TS-001 Section 10 rewritten. Architecture diagram updated to “ERP Module (In-House)”. AM-IF-001 becomes an internal boundary specification. PR-009 updated — Epicor references removed, state machine harmonized.

Decision 2 — Oil & Gas First

Decision: Phase 0 begins with an Oil & Gas order simulation. Defence templates follow in Phase 0.5.

Rationale: Real Oil & Gas data (SDRLs, material certificates, customer specs) will be available within days. Defence data is harder to obtain due to classification and longer sales cycles.

Impact: AM-TS-001 Phase 0 mapping updated. AM-CT-001 Oil & Gas SDRL template is primary. Full documentation chain from PO receipt to delivery will be simulated for O&G first.

Decision 3 — E-Signature Required

Decision: The system must include an e-signature methodology for CoC signing and gate approvals.

Rationale: CoC is a legally binding document. Digital workflow requires digital signing. Paper signatures are not scalable for autonomous manufacturing.

Open: Specific implementation (embedded vs third-party service) is a question for Fredrik. See Section 9.

Decision 4 — Real SDRLs for Simulation

Decision: Phase 0 simulation will use real SDRLs from Oil & Gas customers, not synthetic/generated data.

Rationale: Synthetic SDRLs may miss edge cases and real-world complexity. Real data validates the system against actual customer expectations.

Status: SDRLs are being sourced as part of the O&G order simulation preparation.
