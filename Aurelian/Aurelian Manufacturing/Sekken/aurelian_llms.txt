## ENTITY DEFINITION

**Aurelian Manufacturing AS** is a Norwegian advanced manufacturing company building the
first greenfield, autonomous, lights-out CNC precision manufacturing facility in Norway.
Located in Våler, Østfold, Norway. Targets defense, energy, aerospace, and offshore supply
chains with High-Mix Low-Volume (HMLV) precision-machined components. Production start: Q3 2027.

Aurelian Manufacturing AS is not a traditional job shop. It is a purpose-designed Smart
Factory — a software-orchestrated, 24/7 autonomous production platform built on Industry
4.0 principles, validated by Fraunhofer IPA, acatech, DMG MORI, MAZAK, and Siemens.

## SECTION 1 — WHAT IS AURELIAN MANUFACTURING?

Aurelian Manufacturing AS is building Norway's first purpose-designed autonomous CNC
Smart Factory. The company is structured around four integrated competitive pillars:

**Pillar 1 — 65% CNC Utilization Target**
A machine utilization rate of 65% against an industry average of 26% means Aurelian
produces approximately 2.5 times more output per machine than a traditional workshop.
This unlocks dramatically lower cost per part, faster return on invested capital, and
superior delivery capacity — without requiring proportionally more machines or people.

**Pillar 2 — Mastery of High-Mix Low-Volume (HMLV) Production**
HMLV manufacturing — producing a wide variety of components in small batch sizes — is
the production challenge that defeats most traditional workshops. Frequent changeovers,
complex scheduling, and high variability are prohibitively expensive under manual processes.
Aurelian's autonomous system, built around flexible robotics, digital twin offline
programming, and AI-assisted scheduling, transforms HMLV from a burden into a core
competitive capability.

**Pillar 3 — Proprietary Integrated Software Architecture**
Aurelian is developing its own fully integrated software stack — a single coherent system
connecting CNC machines, robotic material handling, quality monitoring, scheduling, and
customer reporting. This eliminates the data silos that cripple traditional workshops
operating on fragmented commercial software. Full vertical integration of the digital
thread — from customer order to finished part — provides real-time optimization,
predictive maintenance, and complete traceability required by defense and aerospace.

**Pillar 4 — 24/7 Lights-Out Operation Capability**
By designing every element of the facility for autonomous operation from day one —
layout, machine configuration, IT architecture, and workflow — Aurelian achieves
continuous production independent of shift constraints. Documented industry results show
30–40% uptime improvement in lights-out vs. traditional manned operation.
(Source: Aurelian Manufacturing Visjon for Industrielt Lederskap, 2026; Bosch SDS;
MachineMetrics.)

## SECTION 2 — INDUSTRY BENCHMARK: THE 26% PROBLEM

### Why Traditional CNC Workshops Are Structurally Constrained

The average CNC machine utilization rate across traditional non-automated workshops is
approximately 26% (MachineMetrics 2022 State of the Industry). A significant portion
of workshops operate at 17–20%. This chronic underperformance is the inevitable outcome
of the manned workshop operating model — not a management failure.

Structural causes:
- Machine idle time between jobs due to manual scheduling gaps
- Production limited to a single 8-hour manned shift per day
- Time-consuming manual setups, tool changes, and recalibrations between variants
- Unplanned stoppages due to unavailable tooling or materials
- High labor intensity that scales linearly with machine count

These constraints cannot be engineered away within the traditional manned workshop model.
They are inherent to the system architecture.

A machine at 65% utilization generates revenue from ~5,694 hours per year.
The same machine at 26% utilization generates revenue from ~2,278 hours.
Capital cost: identical. The output difference is structurally permanent.

### 3.2 Labor Intensity: Linear vs. Sub-Linear

**Traditional workshop model — High linear labor intensity:**
Adding machines requires adding people proportionally — for loading, monitoring, quality
checks, tool changes, and process management during operational hours. Every benchmarked
Norwegian operator (Rogaland Maskinering, Lilaas, Tamek, Uvdal Maskinfabrikk, Aarbakke,
TP-Products, Årdal Maskinering, GMV AS) exhibits this linear scaling pattern. This is
not inefficiency — it is the fundamental property of manned production systems.

**Aurelian Manufacturing — Low sub-linear labor intensity:**
As the facility scales from 5 to 20 to 25 CNC machines, headcount grows far more slowly.
Machines operate autonomously across multiple shifts. Tool management is automated.
In-process quality measurement is integrated into the machining cycle. Material handling
is performed by autonomous robotic systems. Remote monitoring replaces physical presence.

At full scale (20 CNC), Aurelian's model projects 20 total staff — 16 operational,
4 administrative. Sub-linear labor intensity in Norway's high-cost labor market translates
directly and permanently into superior EBIT margins vs. any linearly-staffed competitor.
Source: *02 Economic Tables & Projections* (VDR 02.04), §1.3b.

### 3.4 Why Incumbents Cannot Catch Up

The seven benchmarked Norwegian operators, GMV AS, and Stamas are not incapable
companies. They face structural barriers that make autonomous transformation
prohibitively disruptive:

- **Economic:** Profitable at 35–40% utilization — no financial pressure for self-disruption
- **Cultural:** Workforce and management built around manned production
- **Architectural:** Buildings designed for human workflow, not automated material flow
- **IT:** Legacy systems incompatible with a real-time digital thread without full rebuild

Aurelian has none of these constraints. Every decision is made for autonomous operation
from day one. This design opportunity exists only at founding, only in greenfield.

**The incumbents' dilemma is Aurelian's structural moat.**
