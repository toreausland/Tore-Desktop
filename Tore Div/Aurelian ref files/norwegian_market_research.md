# Norwegian Manufacturing Market Research

## Defense and Aerospace Sector Overview

### Market Size and Budget
- Norway's defense budget totals USD 8.3 billion (2023)
- Increasing defense spending to reach 2% of GDP by 2026 (currently at 1.7%)
- 30% of defense budget spent on investments (above NATO guideline of 20%)
- Approximately 70% of defense equipment is imported
- The defense and aerospace sector creates more than 120,000 jobs in the Norwegian economy

### Key Players
1. **Kongsberg Defence & Aerospace**
   - Norway's premier supplier of defense and aerospace-related systems
   - Part of the Kongsberg Group
   - Develops technologies from "the depths of the ocean to the edge of space"
   - Over 200 years of expertise in defense technology

2. **Nammo**
   - One of only two large Norwegian defense companies (alongside Kongsberg)
   - Specializes in ammunition, missile technology, and military explosives

### Norwegian Defense Industry Strategy
The Norwegian Government has selected eight focus areas for cooperation between the defense industry and the Norwegian Armed Forces:
1. Command, control, Information Systems
2. System Integration
3. Autonomous Systems
4. Missile Technology
5. Underwater Sensors
6. Ammunition, Target Technology, Remote Controlled Weapon System and Military Explosives
7. Material Technology
8. Life Support for Military Land, Air and Naval Operations Systems

### Investment Plans
- **Army**: Development of Brigade North with four maneuver battalions, equipped with new main battle tanks, mobile air defense systems, and long-range precision fire
- **Navy**: Strengthening with more personnel, frigate upgrades, submarine replacement (in cooperation with Germany, completion around 2030), three new Coast Guard vessels
- **Air Force**: Introduction of F-35 Lightning II fighters, new P-8 Poseidon maritime patrol aircraft, NASAMS II air defense system upgrades
- **Special Forces**: Increasing personnel and adding an operations task group
- **Helicopters**: Upgrading Bell 412 transport helicopters, purchasing six new Sikorsky MH-60R Seahawk helicopters

## Norwegian Space Industry
- Turnover of USD 1 billion
- Considered a growth sector in Norway
- Around 40 public and private companies across the country
- Market leaders: Andøya Space, Telenor, Nammo, and the Kongsberg Group
- Geographical advantage for satellite operations, particularly in Svalbard
- Andøya Spaceport opened in November 2023 for small satellite launches

## Standards and Regulations

### NORSOK Standards (Offshore Industry)
- Developed by the Norwegian petroleum industry
- Ensure adequate safety, value adding, and cost effectiveness
- Currently about 79 national NORSOK standards in active use
- Introduced in 1994 to cut costs and improve competitiveness on the Norwegian continental shelf
- Form an important basis for regulations in Norway's offshore industry
- Managed by Standards Norway in agreement with the Federation of Norwegian Industries, Offshore Norge, and Norwegian Shipowners' Association

### Military Aerospace Standards
- Norway has fully adopted the European Military Airworthiness Requirements (EMAR)
- EMAR serves as the basis for the Airworthiness Regulation in the Norwegian Defence Sector
- Norway is not an EU member but commits to most EU regulations through the European Economic Area (EEA)
- Connected to European Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) standards
- Specific regulations for Approved Aircraft Parts and Requirements for technical skills and competence for maintenance personnel

## Manufacturing Landscape
- Most Norwegian defense companies are relatively small compared to international defense companies
- Larger systems, vessels, and vehicles are usually purchased from foreign companies through industrial cooperation agreements
- Niche product spectrum in the defense industry
- Strong focus on high-quality, specialized manufacturing
- Emphasis on innovation and technological advancement

## Market Opportunities
- High percentage of imported defense equipment (70%) presents opportunities for new manufacturing approaches
- Significant planned investments in defense modernization
- Growing space industry with geographical advantages
- Strong focus on technological innovation and quality
- Need for more efficient and cost-effective manufacturing solutions
- Potential to serve both Norwegian and broader European defense markets
