# Optimal Machine Shop Floor Layout
## 5,000 Square Meter Manufacturing Facility with 500mm Diameter POD Limitation

This document presents the optimal floor layout for the Aurelian project's 5,000 square meter manufacturing facility, with specific focus on the first POD designed for components with a maximum diameter of 500mm.

## Overall Facility Layout Concept

The facility layout is designed based on the principles of lean manufacturing, with an emphasis on efficient material flow, minimized transport distances, and flexible production capabilities. The layout incorporates the following key zones:

1. **Production Zones (PODs)**: Three specialized manufacturing cells
2. **Material Handling & Logistics**: Centralized system with dedicated zones
3. **Quality Control**: Centralized and in-POD inspection areas
4. **Technical & Administrative Areas**: Support functions and offices
5. **Staff Facilities**: Amenities and welfare areas

### Space Allocation

| Area | Size (sq m) | Percentage | Purpose |
|------|-------------|------------|---------|
| Production Floor | 3,200 | 64% | Manufacturing operations (3 PODs) |
| Material Handling | 600 | 12% | Raw material and finished goods storage |
| Quality Control | 300 | 6% | Inspection and testing |
| Technical Offices | 400 | 8% | Engineering, programming, planning |
| Administrative Offices | 300 | 6% | Management, administration, meeting rooms |
| Staff Facilities | 200 | 4% | Locker rooms, break areas, restrooms |

## Detailed POD Layouts

### POD 1: Precision 5-Axis Milling (1,000 sq m)
*Specialized for components up to 500mm diameter*

#### Equipment Configuration
- **Primary Machines**: 3 DMG MORI DMU 65 monoBLOCK 5-axis milling machines
  - Arranged in a triangular configuration for optimal material flow
  - Each with dedicated loading/unloading zone
  - 5m spacing between machines for maintenance access and material handling
  
- **Automation System**: ABB IRB 6700 industrial robots
  - One robot serving each machine
  - Integrated with central material handling system
  - Safety fencing with light curtains and access gates
  
- **Tool Management System**: Centralized for all three machines
  - Tool presetting station
  - Tool storage carousel (300+ tool capacity)
  - Tool transportation system to each machine

- **Inspection Station**: In-process verification
  - CMM with measuring volume of 600mm x 600mm x 500mm
  - Surface finish measurement equipment
  - First article inspection station

#### Space Allocation Within POD 1
- Machine area (3 machines): 450 sq m (150 sq m each including maintenance access)
- Robot cells (3 cells): 180 sq m (60 sq m each)
- Material staging: 150 sq m
- Tool management: 80 sq m
- In-process inspection: 60 sq m
- Operator stations: 30 sq m
- Circulation space: 50 sq m

#### Overhead Crane Coverage
- 5-ton bridge crane covering entire POD 1 area
- Hook height: 5.5 meters
- Bridge span: 20 meters
- Runway length: 50 meters

## Material Flow Design

The facility layout is designed for optimal material flow:

### Raw Material Flow
1. **Receiving**: Dedicated dock on north side
   - Truck unloading area
   - Initial inspection zone
   - Material transfer to internal transport

2. **Storage and Preparation**: Central material handling area
   - Raw material storage racks
   - Cutting and preparation stations
   - Kitting for production

3. **Distribution to PODs**: Via AGV system
   - Scheduled deliveries based on production plan
   - Just-in-time delivery to minimize WIP
   - Return of unused materials

### Production Flow
1. **POD 1 (5-Axis Milling)**:
   - Material loading via robot or crane
   - 5-axis machining operations
   - In-process inspection
   - Transfer to next operation or quality control

2. **POD 2 (Turning)**:
   - Bar loading or chuck loading
   - Turning and mill-turn operations
   - In-process inspection
   - Transfer to next operation or quality control

3. **POD 3 (Waterjet)**:
   - Sheet loading via overhead crane
   - Waterjet cutting operations
   - Part removal and sorting
   - Transfer to next operation or quality control

### Finished Goods Flow
1. **Quality Control**:
   - Final inspection
   - Documentation and certification
   - Approval for shipping

2. **Packaging and Shipping**:
   - Finished goods packaging
   - Order consolidation
   - Shipping preparation
   - Loading at shipping dock on south side

## Overhead Crane System Integration

The facility features a comprehensive overhead crane system designed to support all material handling requirements:

### Main Crane Systems
1. **POD 1 Crane**: 5-ton capacity
   - Coverage: Entire POD 1 area (1,000 sq m)
   - Hook height: 5.5 meters
   - Primary use: Machine loading/unloading, fixture changes

2. **POD 2 Crane**: 3-ton capacity
   - Coverage: Entire POD 2 area (800 sq m)
   - Hook height: 5 meters
   - Primary use: Machine setup, material handling

3. **POD 3 Crane**: 5-ton capacity
   - Coverage: Entire POD 3 area (600 sq m)
   - Hook height: 5 meters
   - Primary use: Sheet loading/unloading

4. **Central Material Handling Crane**: 5-ton capacity
   - Coverage: Central material handling area (800 sq m)
   - Hook height: 5.5 meters
   - Primary use: Material transfer, storage management

### Crane Specifications
- **Bridge Type**: Double-girder overhead bridge cranes
- **Control**: Radio remote control with pendant backup
- **Speed**:
  - Hoist: Variable up to 5 m/min
  - Bridge: Variable up to 40 m/min
  - Trolley: Variable up to 30 m/min
- **Safety Features**:
  - Overload protection
  - Anti-collision systems
  - Audible and visual movement warnings
  - Emergency stop systems

### Crane Infrastructure Requirements
- **Runway Beams**: Integrated into building structure
- **Support Columns**: Positioned to avoid interference with production areas
- **Power Supply**: Enclosed conductor bars
- **Maintenance Access**: Platforms at strategic locations

## Ceiling Height and Overhead Crane Integration

The facility is designed with optimal ceiling heights to accommodate the overhead crane system and provide adequate clearance for all operations:

### Ceiling Height Specifications
- **Overall Building Height**: 9 meters to underside of roof structure
- **Crane Runway Elevation**: 6 meters above floor level
- **Clear Hook Height**: 5-5.5 meters (depending on area)
- **Machine Clearance**: Minimum 1.5 meters above tallest machine
- **Utility Distribution Space**: 1 meter below roof structure

### Overhead Crane Integration
- **Runway Beam Configuration**:
  - POD 1: 20m span x 50m runway
  - POD 2: 16m span x 40m runway
  - POD 3: 15m span x 40m runway
  - Central Material Handling: 20m span x 40m runway

- **Support Structure**:
  - Integrated with building columns
  - Reinforced to handle dynamic loads
  - Vibration isolation from precision machining areas

- **Crane Control Integration**:
  - Centralized monitoring system
  - Load management and tracking
  - Maintenance scheduling based on usage

## Conclusion

This optimal floor layout for the Aurelian project's 5,000 square meter manufacturing facility provides a comprehensive design that accommodates the 500mm diameter limitation for the first POD while ensuring efficient material flow, effective use of overhead crane systems, and integration of all necessary support functions.

The layout emphasizes:
1. **Efficient Production**: Optimized POD configurations for specific manufacturing processes
2. **Effective Material Flow**: Logical progression from receiving through production to shipping
3. **Integrated Material Handling**: Comprehensive overhead crane coverage and AGV system
4. **Support Functions**: Properly sized and positioned quality control, technical, and administrative areas
5. **Future Flexibility**: Design allows for adaptation to changing production requirements

The design meets all specified requirements while providing an optimal working environment for precision manufacturing of aerospace and defense components.
