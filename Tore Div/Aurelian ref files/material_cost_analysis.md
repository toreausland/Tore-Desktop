# Material Cost Analysis for Aerospace and Defense Components

## Common Materials in Aerospace and Defense Manufacturing

### Aluminum Alloys
- **Aluminum 6061-T6**
  - Price: 2.2-2.8 USD/kg (22-28 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: Aircraft structural components, brackets, fixtures
  - Machinability: Excellent (90-100% rating)
  - Material removal rate: Up to 1,500 cm³/min

- **Aluminum 7075-T6**
  - Price: 3.5-4.2 USD/kg (35-42 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: High-strength aircraft components, wing spars, bulkheads
  - Machinability: Good (70-80% rating)
  - Material removal rate: Up to 1,200 cm³/min

- **Aluminum-Lithium Alloys**
  - Price: 8.0-12.0 USD/kg (80-120 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: Lightweight aerospace structures
  - Machinability: Good (65-75% rating)
  - Material removal rate: Up to 1,000 cm³/min

### Titanium Alloys
- **Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5)**
  - Price: 19.0-25.0 USD/kg (190-250 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: Aircraft engine components, structural parts
  - Machinability: Challenging (30-40% rating)
  - Material removal rate: 50-100 cm³/min

- **Ti-5Al-2.5Sn**
  - Price: 22.0-28.0 USD/kg (220-280 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: Cryogenic applications, pressure vessels
  - Machinability: Challenging (25-35% rating)
  - Material removal rate: 40-80 cm³/min

### Stainless Steels
- **17-4 PH Stainless Steel**
  - Price: 6.0-8.0 USD/kg (60-80 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: Aircraft components requiring high strength and corrosion resistance
  - Machinability: Moderate (50-60% rating)
  - Material removal rate: 150-250 cm³/min

- **15-5 PH Stainless Steel**
  - Price: 7.0-9.0 USD/kg (70-90 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: Aircraft fittings, landing gear components
  - Machinability: Moderate (45-55% rating)
  - Material removal rate: 130-220 cm³/min

### Nickel-Based Superalloys
- **Inconel 718**
  - Price: 35.0-45.0 USD/kg (350-450 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: Jet engine components, high-temperature applications
  - Machinability: Poor (15-25% rating)
  - Material removal rate: 20-50 cm³/min

- **Waspaloy**
  - Price: 45.0-60.0 USD/kg (450-600 NOK/kg)
  - Applications: Gas turbine components, high-temperature fasteners
  - Machinability: Very poor (10-20% rating)
  - Material removal rate: 15-40 cm³/min

## Material Cost Optimization Strategies

1. **Vendor Consolidation**: Negotiate volume discounts with key material suppliers
2. **Near-Net-Shape Procurement**: Purchase pre-formed materials closer to final dimensions
3. **Scrap Reduction Programs**: Implement nesting optimization and tool path efficiency
4. **Material Substitution**: Identify opportunities for lower-cost alternative materials
5. **Recycling Programs**: Establish closed-loop recycling for high-value materials
6. **Inventory Optimization**: Implement just-in-time material delivery to reduce carrying costs
7. **Digital Twin Simulation**: Optimize material usage through virtual testing before production

## Conclusion

Material costs represent a significant portion of the overall production costs for aerospace and defense components, particularly for standard components where they can account for up to 42.6% of the selling price in the low case scenario. The implementation of material optimization strategies, particularly improved buy-to-fly ratios and scrap recovery, can significantly impact the profitability of the manufacturing operation.

The best case scenario, with a 25-30% reduction in material costs, would provide a substantial competitive advantage and improve profit margins across all product categories. Even in the medium case scenario, with a 5-10% reduction in material costs, Fortress Manufacturing would maintain competitive material cost structures compared to industry averages.
